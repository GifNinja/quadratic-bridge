Royal Gorge Bridge

History

Dubbed the “Grand Canyon of the Arkansas River” by the first American explorers, the (almost) ten-mile-long Royal Gorge near Cañon City in south-central Colorado has a narrow width of 30 feet at the bottom, with depths occasionally exceeding 1,200 feet. It was the ancient wintering location for Ute Native Americans and was the site of intense corporate feuds such as a battle over railway track. With its iconic red granite formations and extreme height, the Royal Gorge stands as Colorado’s iconic landmark.

Construction Timeline
1906 Congress grants Cañon City the Royal Gorge and surrounding land
1929 Cañon City authorizes the Royal Gorge Bridge and Amusement Company to build the Royal Gorge Bridge
Jun 4th, 1929 Construction begins, financed by Lon P. Piper, the president of the new company, and George E. Cole, a Kansas architect and the bridge’s chief engineer
early Dec 1929 Construction complete
Dec 8th, 1929 The Royal Gorge Bridge officially opens to the public
